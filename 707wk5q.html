11. What type of variable is "Income"?
numerical, interval
numerical, ratio
categorical, nominal
categorical, ordinal

The correct answer is "numerical, interval". Income is an interval measurement because, alas, it could be a negative number. If you measure income as a raw currency amount (eg, $27,933), then the variable is continuous. Careful though! Income is often measured in ranges - like you would see on a survey (eg, $25k to $50k, $50k to $75k, etc.), and if you do it that way, it's an categorical, ordinal variable.

numerical, interval
